The Outbound Playbook

The List Strategy

A dialer is only as good as the fuel you feed it. Pushing exhausted, heavily-dialed data into an aggressive predictive campaign guarantees high abandon rates and burned caller IDs.

  • Isolate Fresh Leads: New data should be dialed aggressively (Speed2Lead) on high ratios.
  • Quarantine Aged Data: Leads with 5+ attempts drop below a 3% connect rate. Move to manual.
  • Timezone Routing: Strict localized dialing windows prevents massive TCPA violations.

Dialer Pacing & Ratios

Finding the sweet spot between agent wait time and customer abandon rates is the core job of the dialer manager. High abandonment directly hurts revenue and invites litigation.

  • The 3% Rule: Legally, your abandon rate must remain under 3% of live answered calls.
  • Adjust by Answer Rate: Dynamically scale ratios based on live pickup probability.
  • Agent Staffing: Predictive algorithms require 10+ agents to function efficiently.

Caller ID (ANI) Health

Carriers (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile) heavily filter traffic. If you over-dial the same phone number, it will be flagged as "Scam Likely" and your connect rates will permanently collapse.

  • Rotate ANIs Weekly: Never use the same Caller ID for more than 500 calls per day.
  • Monitor Answering Machines: Volume spikes here indicate carrier auto-diversion.
  • Use Local Presence: Matching prospect area codes increases pickup probability by 15-20%.

Performance Benchmarks

If you don't know what "good" looks like, you can't optimize for it. Use these general industry benchmarks to quickly gauge if a campaign is bleeding cash.

  • Healthy Contact Rate: > 20% on fresh data, 10-15% on aged lists.
  • Healthy Connect Rate: > 5% means routing and pacing algorithms are highly efficient.
  • Average Handling Time: Aim for 3 to 5 mins. Shorter times imply bad lead quality.
